## §0 Product Identity

**Working name:** Storybook Workshop (TBD — candidates: *Lilbinder*, *StoryForge*, *Kindling*, *Bookwright's Bench*, *Hero Press*).

**One-liner:** A Build-a-Bear-style ritual workshop where a parent crafts a personalized 24-page illustrated children's picture book starring their child as hero — generated end-to-end on-device for privacy, delivered as a free animated digital read-along + a paid printed hardcover mailed to your door. Sold by-the-book or as a recurring grandparent-gift series.

**Three defensible moats:**
1. **Privacy by architecture** — kid's photo/name/address never enter our content-generation pipeline. On-device CLIP vectorizer → opaque pillar IDs sent upstream. Name composited locally at PDF-assembly time. Address only crosses to Lulu API at checkout.
2. **Settler/pachinko provenance** — workshop draws on the existing dynamic settler roster (lead host + cameo attendants) + PreText typography pipeline + World Builder rendering stack. Competitors (Wonderbly, Magic Story) can't replicate without a comparable in-app universe.
3. **Evidence-backed pedagogy** — every parent-facing design knob is tied to a peer-reviewed citation (Symons & Johnson 1997, Stein & Glenn 1979, Bus/van IJzendoorn/Pellegrini 1995, etc.). FTC-safe marketing copy. Real reading researchers on the advisory board (Phase B).

**Build-a-Bear ritual structure** — 7 stations, each a delight ceremony, hosted by a lead settler attendant with cameo settlers at key stations:

| Station | Build-a-Bear analog | Inputs gathered |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Choose Your Story Shape | Pick a bear | theme, occasion, length tier |
| 2. Forge Your Hero | Stuff the bear | photo → pillar ID via on-device CLIP |
| 3. The Wish Moment | Heart ceremony | dedication text + voice clip |
| 4. Name Your Cast | Name the bear | hero name, sidekick settler, supporting cast, locale |
| 5. Dress Your Story | Dress the bear | art style + extras |
| 6. The Seal | Birth certificate | preview, redo, consent gate |
| 7. Take It Home | Take home | free digital, paid print |

- `/dashboard/storybook-workshop/library` — parent's library of past + in-progress books
- `/storybook-workshop/preview/{shortcode}` — public shareable digital book read-along (email-gated past page 4)

**Workshop scene** — rendered via existing World Builder painted_2d as a back-room print-shop aesthetic (workbench, printing press, paper stacks, lamps). Settler attendants at stations. Uses existing `AsciiSceneRenderer` + `CompositorOverlay` + `PretextCompositor`. The scene IS the UX — stations are places, not forms.

**Kernel integration:**
- `kidsContentSafetyManifest` (new, colocated, background priority)
- `inference.generate.requirableBy` extended with: `storybook-workshop-author`, `storybook-workshop-vocab`, `storybook-workshop-prompts`, `storybook-workshop-grammar`, `storybook-workshop-safety`

**Standalone-product framing:** every service lives under `services/storybook-workshop/` (or new kernel manifests). No deep coupling to fishbowl. Future extraction: copy this subtree + shared kernel infra (extracted as npm package) → standalone repo.

## §2 Workshop Flow — 7 Stations

**Pre-flow: Kid picker.** Parent's roster of kids stored local IDB. "+ New Hero" → photo + name + age + 1-line about. Resume in-progress book draft per kid.

**Standard mode** = the 7 stations below. **Advanced mode** = standard + 3 additional stations (§7.6 below).

### Station 1 — Choose Your Story Shape
- 12 theme cards: bedtime / first-day / lost-and-found / overcoming-fear / new-baby-arrives / kindness / adventure / curiosity / friendship / sibling-rivalry / saying-goodbye / silly-quest.
- Special occasion chip: birthday / holiday / gift / just-because.
- **Length picker** (4 tiers): Bedtime ~16pp / Standard ~24pp ← default / Adventure ~32pp / Saga ~48pp.
- Length default auto-picked from kid's age-band (toddler→Bedtime, preschool→Standard, grade-school→Adventure).
- **Ehri-phase self-assessment** (5 questions, optional — see §7.3) — sets reading phase used for vocab/sentence-length calibration.
- Carries: `{ theme, occasion, targetSpreads, ehriPhase }`.

### Station 2 — Forge Your Hero
- Photo capture (camera or file picker).
- **On-device WASM CLIP** (`Xenova/clip-vit-base-patch32` via `@xenova/transformers` 2.17.2 from CDN) → 512-dim embedding (~200ms desktop, ~500ms phone).
- **Local cosine match** against pillar-library manifest (fetched once at session start). Top-K=8 with age-band re-rank.
- Animated "spinning archetype wheel" lands on top-8 pillars. Parent: "More like this / Different vibe" to refine.
- Parent confirms `pillarId`.
- **Fallback path** (WASM init fails): explicit consent → POST photo to `/api/storybook-workshop/vectorize` → server-side CLIP → vector returned → photo discarded synchronously. Disclosure inline.
- **Manual-pick escape** always available: skip photo entirely → curated archetype grid filtered by age.
- Carries: `pillarId`.

### Station 3 — The Wish Moment
- Free-text dedication OR voice recording (≤30s, local IDB only) OR template pick.
- Settler-suggested templates: *"I hope you stay as curious as you are right now."*
- All text routes through PrivacyFilter + KidsContentSafety before storage.
- Carries: `dedicationText`, `voiceClipBlob?`, `templateId?`.

### Station 4 — Name Your Cast
- Hero name (defaults to kid's name from profile).
- Sidekick settler (dropdown from user's roster + 3 theme-matched suggestions).
- Pets / sibs (from kid profile or new).
- Locale (kid's hometown or fantasy place — embedded → matched to World Builder biome).
- Carries: `{ heroName, sidekickSettlerId, supportingCast[], localeBiome }`.

### Station 5 — Dress Your Story
- 10-12 art-style grid showing same matched pillar in each style: pixel-32, painted-2d, watercolor, ghibli-ish, low-poly, ink-line, crayon, gouache, manga, claymation, chalkboard, paper-cutout.
- Optional extras: author byline (kid credited as author), endpaper pattern, cover badge ("Birthday Edition" etc.), **"easier-reading mode"** (spacing/leading/sans-serif per §7.1 #9), dialogic prompt toggle (default ON).
- Carries: `{ artStyle, authorByline?, endpaper?, coverBadge?, easierReadingMode, dialogicPromptsEnabled }`.

### Station 6 — The Seal
- Animated generation sequence (~30-60s): story author → scene brief per beat → World Builder render per scene → PreText typography composite per spread → BookAssembler.
- "Birth certificate" stamps the book with: unique book ID, kid's name, date, lead-settler signature. Animated wax-seal ceremony.
- **Preview** — parent flips through digital book (page-turn animation). Can:
  - **Per-scene redo** — re-runs author for one scene + re-renders that scene's spreads (~10s)
  - **Style swap globally** — re-renders all spreads in new style, story preserved (~15s with concurrency)
  - **Pillar swap** — re-renders all spreads with new pillar (~15s)
  - **Beat compress/expand** — drop/add a scene in a beat, reflow budget
  - **Insert spread** — add a quiet moment inside a scene
  - **Story reroll** — full pipeline rerun (~30s)
  - **Page-budget slider** at top — live re-render at new length (~30s)
- **Consent gate** (mandatory, both required):
  - "I've reviewed every spread."
  - "I understand this is a personalized print — I can't return it for taste reasons."
- Settler banter: *"This is your last chance to redo anything. I'll wait."*

### Station 7 — Take It Home
- **Free digital** — instant. PDF (screen-res, watermark-free) + ePub3 with dedication-audio media-overlay + animated web read-along (PreText typography animations + voice-over). Email gate after page 4 of preview (§8).
- **Order printed copy** — format picker (hardcover 8×8 / softcover 8×8 / saddle-stitch for Bedtime). Shipping address. Stripe checkout. POST to Lulu Direct.
- **Series gift CTA** — *"Make this a 12-book year — let grandma fund it"* → §6.4 gift flow.
- Pricing v1: $34.99 hardcover Standard 24pp (flagship), $19.99 softcover, $44.99 hardcover Saga 48pp. Live Lulu shipping quote. Board book deferred to v2.

### Cross-cutting
- Each station = full-screen scene + back-arrow + progress bar (7 dots, 10 in advanced mode).
- Animated walk-transition between stations (workshop layout).
- Draft persisted to IDB after every station (`storybook-workshop-drafts-v1` store); resumable.
- "Quick Mode" toggle collapses ceremony to 4-screen wizard. Same backend.
- Anonymous local telemetry: drop-off, time-per-station, format conversion. No PII off-device.

## §3 Content Pipeline (Coordinator, Not Renderer)

The Skill coordinates; **all image rendering happens upstream at World Builder**. Skill never owns pixels.

### 3.1 Outbound payload to World Builder

```ts
{
  pillarId: 4827,                      // opaque integer, anonymous
  style: 'painted-2d',                  // enum
  sidekickSettlerId: 'ada-7f3...',     // existing public roster ID
  supportingCast: [pillarId|settlerId], // opaque IDs only
  localeBiome: 'seaside',               // enum
  sceneBrief: 'The hero finds...',     // PrivacyFilter + KidsContentSafety pre-scrubbed prose
  beatNumber: 4,
  sceneIndexInBeat: 2,
  spreadCount: 1..5,                   // multi-spread continuity
  cameraSequence: 'establishing'|'pan'|'follow'|'tight-on-hero'|'reveal',
  layout: 'spread'
}
```

**No PII crosses this boundary.** Name composited locally at PDF assembly (§3.7).

### 3.2 Pillar library (upstream, pre-rendered)
- Owned by World Builder. 5,000 archetype kid avatars × 12 styles ≈ 60,000 images (~30 GB CDN).
- Axes: hair (8) × skin tone (6) × eye (4) × age-band (3) × clothing-vibe (5) × extras (glasses/freckles).
- `GET /api/world/pillar-library/manifest` → `{pillarId, axes, age-band, embedding}[]` (~5 MB JSON, fetched once per workshop session, cached).
- `GET /api/world/pillar-library/{pillarId}/preview?style=...` → preview PNG per pillar × style.
- **Pre-launch deliverable** for World Builder team. Goal `storybook-workshop-pillar-library-assets` (Wave 3) drives generation.

### 3.3 On-device CLIP vectorizer
- `@xenova/transformers` 2.17.2 via CDN (cannot go through Vite optimizer per project gotcha).
- `Xenova/clip-vit-base-patch32`, WASM CPU backend.
- Output: 512-dim image vector. Kid photo deleted from memory + IDB after single forward pass.
- Fallback `/api/storybook-workshop/vectorize` endpoint: rate-limited per IP, no auth, no logging beyond aggregate counter, photo discarded synchronously after CLIP, stateless, TLS only.

### 3.4 Pillar match (local, top-K cosine)
- Kid embedding × pillar embeddings → cosine sim → top-K.
- Re-rank: age-band match weight.
- **Only pillar IDs ever leave the device.**

### 3.5 Story generation (Pixar 7-beat → scene tree)
- Single LLM call via `kernel.connect('inference.generate', 'storybook-workshop-author')`.
- Schema: hierarchical scene tree (each beat → N scenes → M spreads, total = `targetSpreads`).
- System prompt cached per kid profile (Stage 7+ LLM Compression rules).
- User message carries inputs (theme, occasion, cast, locale, length, Ehri phase).
- Stein-Glenn story-grammar enforcement embedded in system prompt + `StoryGrammarValidator` post-pass.
- Tier-2 vocabulary planner pre-selects 3-5 target words per book.
- KidsContentSafety gates every output. PrivacyFilter scrubs prose for any PII that leaked in.
- 2-retry on safety/grammar miss → template fallback via `literarySpineBank`.

### 3.6 Age-band vocabulary calibration
- 3 bands: toddler (2-4) / preschool (4-6) / grade-school (6-9).
- Per-band caps: sentence length (8/14/22 words), syllable cap (3/4/6), paragraph length (2/4/6 sentences).
- `AgeBandCalibrator` post-LLM: Flesch-Kincaid pass. Over-cap → single regen with "simpler vocab" addendum.
- Calibrated by Ehri phase (Station 1 self-assessment).

### 3.7 Scene rendering (per scene → 1..N spreads)
- One `POST /api/world/scene` per scene (not per spread — WB returns N spreads in one call for continuity).
- WB returns PNG array (16×8 inches @ 300dpi, gutter + bleed marks).
- **Upstream dep:** WB must support `spreadCount > 1` per scene with continuity. Goal `storybook-workshop-worldbuilder-upstream-changes` (Wave 3) drives.

### 3.8 PreText typography composite (per spread)
- BookSpreadSurfaceAdapter extends existing PretextCompositor pattern.
- Per-beat default emotional-typography effect (12 modes — see §7.5).
- Text wraps around scene focal point via `PretextFlowEngine`.
- Hand-set typography per Ehri phase (chunky 24-36pt for pre-readers, traditional 14-18pt for fluent).
- Tier-2 words subtly emphasized (color/weight).
- Dialogic prompts rendered as `Speech` element in margins.
- **Digital read-along**: live animated per-char physics via `PretextTextCanvas.svelte`.
- **Print PDF**: static frame captured at visually-strongest animation moment.

### 3.9 Local PDF/ePub assembly with name composition
- `BookAssembler` runs locally. THIS IS THE ONLY STEP THAT TOUCHES KID'S NAME (overlay onto WB-rendered PNGs).
- 9+ PNG inputs (cover + endpaper + title + dedication + N spreads + back-cover).
- PDF generation via `pdf-lib` or `@react-pdf/renderer` → CMYK 300dpi bleed-marked.
- **Lulu-spec validator** runs pre-checkout — reject early before charging.
- ePub3 with dedication-audio media-overlay.
- Web read-along page assembled separately (animated PreText canvas + voice-over + page-turn).

### 3.10 Redo affordances
- Per-scene: re-runs §3.5 (that scene only) + §3.7 (1 WB call) + §3.8 + §3.9 (~10s).
- Style swap: §3.7 (parallel) + §3.8 + §3.9 (~15s).
- Pillar swap: §3.7 + §3.8 + §3.9 (~15s).
- Story reroll: full pipeline (~30s).
- All cached per-version in IDB. Undo restores prior render.

### 3.11 Draft persistence + telemetry
- IDB store `storybook-workshop-drafts-v1` keyed by `{kidId, draftId}`.
- Resume URL: `/dashboard/storybook-workshop?draftId=...`.
- Draft TTL 30 days, auto-purged.
- Telemetry: per-station drop-off, time, redo counts, format conversion. Local-only, surfaced at `/debug/storybook-workshop`.

## §4 Privacy & Safety

### 4.1 KidsContentSafetyService (new kernel manifest)
- `services/kids-content-safety/KidsContentSafetyService.ts` + 4 backends (webgpu/wasm/ollama/stub).
- Pattern mirrors `PrivacyFilterService`.
- Categories: `violence`, `fear-permanent`, `sexual-adult`, `substance`, `religious-political`, `scary-unresolved`, `bigotry`.
- Default classifier: small DistilBERT fine-tuned on age-banded kid-content corpus, WASM-ONNX ~30 MB bundle. Stub-mode keyword list ships day 1.
- Kernel: `kidsContentSafetyManifest` (colocated, background, volatile), capability `kids-content.scan`, caller allowlist `storybook-workshop-*`.
- Returns `{passed, categories, confidence}`.
- Audit ring buffer (1000 entries, local-only).

### 4.2 Five enforcement gates
1. Story-generation output (every beat + title + back-cover blurb).
2. Dedication text (parent's free input).
3. Voice-clip transcript.
4. Scene-brief outbound to World Builder.
5. Cover badge / endpaper / extras (any free-text input).

### 4.3 PII handling

| Data | Location | Crosses network? | Audit purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Kid photo | Local IDB ephemeral (cleared post-vectorize) | No (happy) / Yes one-shot fallback | `kid_photo_local` / `kid_photo_vectorize_fallback` |
| Kid embedding | Local | No | `kid_embedding_local` |
| Kid name | Local IDB, in PDF blob | Lulu only (PDF text + dedication + cover) | `book_fulfillment` |
| Kid age | Local | No (age band crosses, not raw age) | `kid_age_local` |
| Dedication text | Local IDB, in PDF | Lulu inside PDF | `book_fulfillment` |
| Voice clip | Local IDB | No (embedded ePub locally, never uploaded) | `voice_clip_local` |
| Shipping address | Local IDB + Stripe + Lulu | Yes | `book_fulfillment` |
| Pillar ID | Local + World Builder | Yes, opaque integer | `pillar_lookup` |
| Scene brief | Local + World Builder | Yes, scrubbed prose | `scene_render` |
| Settler IDs | Local + World Builder | Yes, public IDs | `scene_render` |

All purposes registered in kernel `purpose.check` allowlist. `book_fulfillment` is the ONLY purpose authorized to carry name+address.

### 4.4 Photo capture + WASM-fallback consent
- Happy path: WASM init → photo in Svelte store → CLIP → vector kept + photo deleted from IDB before Station 3 transition. Settler banter: *"Got it. Photo's gone from our memory now."*
- Fallback: WASM init fails → modal: *"Your device can't run the vectorizer locally. Want us to do it on our server, one-time, photo deleted immediately?"* + disclosure. Opt-out: skip photo, manual grid pick.

### 4.5 COPPA
- Product is for parents/grandparents purchasing for children. UX adult-targeted (checkout, shipping forms).
- Kid's data collected from parent acting as guardian. Explicit consent banner at first kid-profile creation.
- No accounts for kids. No social. No third-party data brokering.
- Parental right-to-delete: `/storybook-workshop/library` per-kid "Delete kid + all data + all unprinted books" button.
- Privacy policy + COPPA-K + ToS deliverables before launch.

### 4.6 No-chain, no-commons, no-lexicon
- Nothing from workshop touches Flow chain, commons, or per-settler lexicon.
- Skill registry entry is the only upstream surface (public Skill manifest, no per-user data).

## §5 Fulfillment

### 5.1 Provider routing
| Format | Provider | Min pp | Max pp |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hardcover casewrap (8×8, 8.5×8.5, 10×10) | Lulu Direct | 24 | 800 |
| Softcover perfect-bound (8×8, 8.5×8.5) | Lulu Direct | 32 | 740 |
| Saddle-stitch (Bedtime tier) | Lulu Direct | 4 | 48 |
| Board book | **Deferred to v2** — Pint Size Productions (no public API, US-only) | 16 | 24 |

V1 ships Lulu-only. Workshop hides board-book option in v1.

### 5.2 Lulu Direct integration
- Service: `services/storybook-workshop/fulfillment/LuluFulfillmentService.ts`.
- OAuth2 client-credentials → JWT cached server-side. Backend proxies (no client-side API key).
- Backend `POST /api/storybook-workshop/order`:
  1. Validate PDF against Lulu print-spec (page count, bleed, trim, CMYK, fonts). Reject pre-charge with parent-readable error.
  2. Validate address via Lulu shipping-cost endpoint → returns shipping options + cost + ETA.
  3. Stripe PaymentIntent for `book_cost + shipping + tax`.
  4. On `payment_intent.succeeded` webhook → POST Lulu `/print-jobs/`.
  5. Persist `kid_id → book_id → lulu_job_id` to backend DB.
  6. Lulu webhook → `POST /api/storybook-workshop/lulu-webhook` → status update + parent email at key events.

### 5.3 Order lifecycle
`pending_payment → paid → submitted_to_lulu → in_production → shipped → delivered`
+ branches: `cancelled_pre_production`, `failed_validation`, `lulu_error_recoverable`, `lulu_error_terminal`, `lost_in_transit`.

Each transition timestamped, audit-logged, surfaced in parent's library view.

### 5.4 Cancellation
- Lulu cancel window ~60-90 min post-submission. Workshop exposes "Cancel order" button while state is `submitted_to_lulu`. Past window: button disables.
- Full Stripe refund on successful cancel.

### 5.5 Error handling
- PDF validation pre-Stripe: parent stays on checkout, error inline, no charge.
- Lulu submission fail post-charge: 3× exponential backoff retry, on full fail → auto Stripe refund + parent email + ops review.
- Lulu reports defect mid-job: webhook captures, parent emailed, auto re-print at our cost.
- Lost in transit: parent reports → ops re-submits at our cost.
- Reprint reserve baked into unit cost (~2% expected).

### 5.6 Shipping geography
V1: US + EU + UK + AU + CA. Lulu auto-routes to closest print site. Other geos: email-capture for marketing.

### 5.7 Quality Guarantee (NOT satisfaction guarantee)
- **Covered:** print defects (smearing, mis-registration, binding fail, missing pages, paper damage, delamination), wrong content delivered, lost/damaged in transit, color outside Lulu spec.
- **Not covered:** "I don't like the story" / illustrations / pillar match — preview-approval consent gate captures this pre-charge.
- 30-day window from delivery. Photo evidence required for defect claims.
- Quality issue → free reprint OR full refund, parent picks.
- No returns (POD personalized = no resale value).

### 5.8 Customer service
V1: `hello@<product>.com` + in-app contact form prefilled with order ID + last 10 audit entries. Manual ops initially.

## §7 Pedagogy + PreText Typography + Advanced Mode

### 7.1 Ten evidence-backed knobs (citations baked in)
| # | Knob | UI | Citation |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Hero personalization | mandatory (S4) | Symons & Johnson 1997 (d≈0.5 recall) |
| 2 | Age-tuned sentence length | invisible auto | Brown 1973, Owens 2016 |
| 3 | Ehri-phase decoding match | S1 self-assessment | Ehri 2005 |
| 4 | Dialogic margin prompts | S5 toggle, default ON | Whitehurst 1988, Mol & Bus 2011 |
| 5 | Tier-2 vocab seeding (3-5 words, spaced) | invisible auto | Beck/McKeown/Kucan 2013, Nagy 1985 |
| 6 | Rhyme/alliteration density | S5 slider, default HIGH ≤5 | Bryant 1990, NRP 2000 |
| 7 | Stein-Glenn story-grammar | invisible (LLM constraint + validator) | Stein & Glenn 1979 |
| 8 | Word-image congruence | invisible (scene-brief = text content) | Paivio 1986, Takacs 2015 |
| 9 | Spacing/leading "easier-reading mode" | S5 toggle | Marinus 2016, Schneps 2013 (spacing IS the active ingredient — NO OpenDyslexic branding) |
| 10 | Bedtime read-aloud length presets | S1 length picker (8/12/16 min) | Bus/vanIJzendoorn/Pellegrini 1995 |

### 7.2 Refused asks (visible in FAQ)
- VAK / learning styles → Pashler 2008 demolished
- OpenDyslexic font marketing → Marinus 2016 (spacing helps, glyph doesn't)
- Therapeutic claims ("treats anxiety") → FTC risk + bibliotherapy weak for kids
- IQ/genius claims → no defensible effect size
- Bilingual cognitive advantage → Lehtonen 2018 meta failed

### 7.3 Ehri-phase self-assessment (S1, 5 questions)
1. Recognizes own name in writing?
2. Knows what sound 'b' makes?
3. Sounds out simple words like 'cat'?
4. Reads short sentences alone?
5. Reads chapter books without pictures?
→ scored to Ehri phase → adjusts vocab + sentence length + rhyme density + decodable-word fraction.

### 7.4 Embedded dialogic prompts (PEER/CROWD)
- Default ON. Generated by LLM as 7-beat author runs, calibrated to age + theme + beat emotional arc.
- CROWD types: Completion, Recall, Open-ended, Wh-question, Distancing.
- PEER framework: Prompt → Evaluate → Expand → Repeat.
- Margin box in digital read-along (scatter-in animation). Italic side-note in print.

### 7.5 PreText typography pipeline (per-beat per-spread)
- Every spread composited through `PretextCompositor` (existing infra).
- Per-beat default emotional-typography effect:
  | Beat | Effect | Why |
  |---|---|---|
  | Setup | flow | calm |
  | Catalyst | bounce | something arrives |
  | Debate | wave | uncertainty |
  | Midpoint | magnetic | world pulls together |
  | Trial | glitch | conflict |
  | Climax | dragon / vortex | peak |
  | Resolution | rise | release |
- Digital read-along: live animated per-char physics via `PretextTextCanvas.svelte`.
- Print PDF: static frame captured at strongest moment of animation.
- Text wraps around scene focal point via `PretextFlowEngine`.
- Hand-set typography per Ehri phase.
- New `BookSpreadSurfaceAdapter.ts` extends existing pattern (FeedSurfaceAdapter etc.). Element types: GridBlock / Prose / Label / Speech.

### 7.6 Advanced Mode (power-user, optional)

**Station 1.5 — Pedagogy Override**: all 10 knobs as direct controls + citations under each.

**Station 3.5 — Wish Engineering**: multi-recording (parent + grandma + sibling), audio-track upload, custom inscription with PreText effect picker, multi-author byline.

**Always-visible inspectors in advanced mode at S6 preview:**

## §8 Marketing Funnel & Email Automation

### 8.1 Email-gated digital preview (the conversion mechanic)
- Anonymous visitor lands at `/dashboard/storybook-workshop` → builds a book without signup.
- After Station 6 generation completes, free-tier preview shows: **cover + dedication + first 4 spreads + back-cover blurb**, fully animated PreText read-along.
- Beyond page 4: **soft email gate**. *"Enter your email to read the full book (free) — we'll send you a shareable link."*
- On email submission: full digital book unlocks immediately + email sent with `/storybook-workshop/preview/{shortcode}` shareable link.
- Email tagged in CRM with: kid age band, theme picked, length tier, anonymized pillar archetype family (no PII).

### 8.2 Email lifecycle
**T+0** (immediate): "Your book is ready" + read-along link + Buy Print CTA + Share-with-grandparents CTA.
**T+1h** (no purchase): Soft nudge — "Did you share it with anyone yet?" + grandma-gifts-back angle.
**T+24h** (no purchase): "Make this a printed keepsake — $34.99 + free shipping with code BEDTIME10" (first-time 10% off).
**T+72h** (still no purchase): Subscription pitch — "What if Grandma made this a series?" + gift-flow link.
**T+7d**: Educational content — "5 things research says about reading to your kid" + 1 citation per item + soft Buy CTA.
**T+14d**: Last-chance — "Your book expires in 16 days unless you save it. Print one now, or download the PDF for $4.99."
**T+30d**: Book digital expiry warning + "We saved your kid's profile, come back any time."

All emails: unsubscribe link, opt-out per-type (transactional/marketing/educational), GDPR-compliant footer.

### 8.3 Abandoned cart automation
- Parent reaches Station 7 checkout but doesn't pay → email at T+1h ("You were one click away — here's $5 off"), T+24h, T+72h with escalating incentives.
- Per-kid abandonment respected: don't spam parents with multiple incomplete drafts.

### 8.4 Grandparent-referral viral loop
- Every read-along link `<product>.com/r/{shortcode}` has a prominent "Order this for {kid_name}" CTA + a smaller "Make one for your own grandkid" CTA.
- Grandma clicks → lands at `/gift` pre-filled with kid metadata for the recipient.
- Tracking: anonymous referral chain (link → click → purchase) attributed to source `shortcode`.
- Bonus: parent gets a $5 credit when their share-link converts a grandparent purchase.

### 8.5 Birthday cron
- Per kid profile, parent sets birthday. 6 weeks pre-birthday email auto-fires to parent AND any registered grandparent (parent opt-in to grandparent register).
- Subject: *"Eli's birthday is in 6 weeks — make this her best year."*
- CTA: pre-filled series gift flow.

### 8.6 Educational content drip (low-pressure brand-building)
- Weekly email to opted-in subscribers: 1 reading-research finding + 1 citation + 1 product-tie.
- Examples: "Why naming your child in the story actually helps memory (Symons & Johnson 1997)" → Buy CTA.
- Builds advisory-council brand credibility over time.

### 8.7 Email infrastructure
- Resend or Postmark for transactional + marketing (both COPPA-K compliant, GDPR-safe).
- Email templating: per-kid placeholders (name, age, last-book theme) but never include the actual kid photo or rendered book interior in the email (only the shareable link).
- Open-rate + click-through tracked anonymously per cohort (no per-user behavioral profiling).

### 8.8 Landing page surfaces
- `<product>.com/` — Hero + the 3 moats + CTA "Make Your First Book (Free)"
- `<product>.com/research` — The 10 evidence knobs with citation cards + advisory council bios (when recruited)
- `<product>.com/gift` — Grandma's gift flow entry
- `<product>.com/privacy` — Network-tab proof + on-device architecture explainer
- `<product>.com/r/{shortcode}` — Public read-along (email-gated past page 4)
